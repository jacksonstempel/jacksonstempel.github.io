About

I'm an undergraduate researcher in computational biochemistry at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, working at the UT/ORNL Center for Molecular Biophysics. My research focuses on benchmarking machine learning approaches for molecular docking, particularly comparing modern ML methods like Boltz against traditional approaches for lipid-ligand pose prediction.

I'm interested in how AI can accelerate drug discovery and our understanding of molecular interactions, with a particular focus on membrane proteins and lipid binding systems.

Current Research

Benchmarking Lipid-Ligand Docking Accuracy
Comparing Boltz (ML-based) vs AutoDock Vina across curated protein datasets. Preliminary results show median RMSD of 1.30 Å vs 2.00 Å, suggesting ML approaches may significantly improve accuracy for lipid binding predictions.
Ecological Field Research (Summer 2024)
NSF IRES program at University of Parakou, investigating ant-insect interactions in West African mahogany forests using structural equation modeling.
